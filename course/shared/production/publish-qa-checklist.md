# Publish QA Checklist

## How To Use

- Run this checklist after editing is complete and before publish.
- Review one lesson at a time.
- Mark every item `pass`, `fix`, or `not applicable`.

## 1. Technical Accuracy

- Cursor UI shown in the video matches the current product closely enough.
- Any pricing or subscription mention was checked against the official source on recording day.
- Any provider mention such as `Vercel`, `Render`, `Netlify`, `Railway`, or `Fly.io` is still accurate enough to teach the concept.
- Prompts shown on screen match the stored prompt assets.
- Commands shown on screen are correct and reproducible.
- No outdated settings path, menu path, or button label is taught as if it were guaranteed permanent.

## 2. Script Accuracy

- The spoken narration matches the approved script master.
- Scene order matches the script.
- Scene IDs and lesson title are consistent across files.
- Claims about models, pricing, or capabilities are framed as guidance, not rigid facts, unless verified.

## 3. Pedagogy

- The lesson starts with a clear problem or use case.
- The learner sees the outcome early.
- The lesson teaches one main topic rather than many mixed topics.
- The pace is suitable for a mixed audience.
- Jargon is either explained or intentionally left in English with context.
- The lesson ends with one clear action item.

## 4. Screen Recording Quality

- Cursor window is readable on laptop-sized screens.
- Terminal font size is readable.
- Mouse movement is intentional and not distracting.
- No sensitive information appears on screen.
- Notifications, private tabs, and unrelated files are hidden.
- Zooms and cuts do not hide important interactions.

## 5. Avatar / Voice Quality

- Voice tone matches the course style.
- Speaking pace is steady.
- Technical terms are pronounced consistently.
- No sentence sounds robotic, clipped, or rushed.
- Avatar presence is helpful and not blocking the product demo.

## 6. Captions

- Captions are synced to the final audio.
- Terms like `Cursor`, `Agent`, `Plan mode`, `MCP`, `Vercel`, and `GitHub` are written consistently.
- Lines are short enough to read comfortably.
- No obvious punctuation or spelling issues remain.
- Hebrew directionality renders correctly.

## 7. Course Consistency

- Naming is consistent with earlier lessons.
- Terminology matches the glossary.
- Prompt structure feels consistent with other lessons in the series.
- Theme, framing, and visual rhythm match the rest of the course.

## 8. Localization Readiness

- Script master exists and is final.
- Caption master exists or can be generated cleanly from the script.
- No hard-coded Hebrew inside the screen capture unless intentional.
- Scene IDs are stable enough for translation and caption retiming.
- Any culturally specific references are easy to adapt later.

## 9. Publish Gate

- Lesson file naming follows project convention.
- Thumbnail text matches the lesson topic.
- Lesson description is accurate.
- Source assets are archived.
- Final exported video opens and plays correctly.
